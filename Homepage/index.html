Project AlgoVerse

Project AlgoVerse is a webpage made to perform and illustrate some commonly used, yet very important algorithms that have many applications in pathfinding, optimization, AI, Machine Learning, etc..

AlgoVerse is made by Emon Ashraf, Muthanna Belal, and Al-Samet Ous of Higher IT School, Tomsk State University.

Here is a short introduction about the algorithms implemented in this project:

A* (A Star)

A* algorithm is a popular search algorithm used for finding the shortest path between two points in a graph. It combines elements of breadth-first search and Dijkstra's algorithm to efficiently explore the graph. By using a heuristic function, A* intelligently prioritizes nodes to explore, guiding the search towards promising paths. The algorithm maintains a set of evaluated nodes, selecting the one with the lowest cost at each step. A* is widely used in fields such as robotics, game development, and transportation planning for its efficiency and optimality in pathfinding problems.

Clusterization

Clusterization algorithms, also known as clustering algorithms, are used in unsupervised machine learning to group similar data points together based on their characteristics. These algorithms aim to identify inherent patterns and structures within the data without prior knowledge of the classes or labels. They are used in various fields such as data mining, pattern recognition, and image analysis. Popular clustering algorithms include K-means, hierarchical clustering, and density-based clustering, each with its own strengths and weaknesses. The choice of algorithm depends on the nature of the data and the desired outcome of the clustering task.

Genetic Algorithm

Genetic algorithms are effective in solving the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP), which aims to find the shortest route visiting a set of cities. The algorithm starts with a population of random routes, evaluates their fitness based on total distance, and selects fitter routes for reproduction. Through crossover and mutation, new routes are generated with inherited traits. The process continues, gradually improving routes until a satisfactory solution is found. The best route in the final population represents the optimal solution to the TSP.

Ant Algorithm

The Ant Algorithm is a metaheuristic optimization algorithm inspired by the behavior of ants. It mimics the foraging behavior of ants searching for food by using pheromone trails. The algorithm involves a group of virtual ants that iteratively explore the solution space, depositing pheromone trails to mark promising paths. Ants follow the trails and make decisions based on local information and the intensity of pheromone concentrations. The pheromone trails are updated dynamically based on the quality of solutions found. This algorithm is commonly used for solving combinatorial optimization problems, such as the Traveling Salesman Problem.

Decision-tree

The Decision Tree Algorithm is a supervised machine learning algorithm used for classification and regression tasks. It constructs a tree-like model where each internal node represents a feature or attribute, and each leaf node represents a class label or a prediction. The algorithm recursively splits the data based on different features, aiming to create branches that maximize the information gain or decrease in impurity. Decision trees are interpretable, easy to understand, and can handle both categorical and numerical data. They are widely used in various domains due to their versatility and ability to handle complex decision-making scenarios.

Neural Network

Neural Networks are a type of machine learning model inspired by the structure and functioning of biological neural networks in the human brain. They consist of interconnected layers of artificial neurons (nodes) that process and transmit information. In the context of digit recognition, neural networks can be trained to identify and classify handwritten or digital digits. They learn to recognize patterns and features in the input data by adjusting the weights and biases of the network through a process called backpropagation. Neural networks have achieved remarkable accuracy in digit recognition tasks and are widely used in various applications such as optical character recognition (OCR) and automated form processing.
